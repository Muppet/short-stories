Mark swallowed dryly… “But, that’s just a metaphor”. “Is ɪᴛ?” The Clerk gazed at him quizzically. Or as quizzically as an ever shifting number of eyes could. Looking back toward the Lamb, Mark had the sinking realisation that this was indeed his “saviour”. Although the goat legs made him think of someone else. Was this really why he was referred to as the Lamb? He also noticed that the source of light in this place came not from the sky, but from the fiery beings, the burning lake, and a great light at the centre of the black marble city. There, he beheld what he realised must surely be the burning light of God. It singed his eyes to stare at it, but he found himself unable to look away, all too late realising that his eyes had been burned from his sockets under its intensity, yet curiously, he felt no pain. In his mind, he finished the passage from Revelation 21:4 “He will wipe away every tear from their eyes, and death shall be no more, neither shall there be mourning, nor crying, nor pain any more, for the former things have passed away.” The Clerk seemed to perk up as he thought this. “Sᴇᴇ, ɴᴏᴡ ʏᴏᴜ ɢᴇᴛ ɪᴛ. Iᴛ ᴊᴜsᴛ ɢᴏᴇs ᴏɴ ᴀɴᴅ ᴏɴ ғᴏʀᴇᴠᴇʀ. Jᴜsᴛ ʟɪᴋᴇ ʏᴏᴜ ᴘᴇᴏᴘʟᴇ ᴛᴇʟʟ ʏᴏᴜʀsᴇʟғ ᴅᴜʀɪɴɢ ʟɪғᴇ. Iғ ʏᴏᴜ ᴡᴏʀsʜɪᴘ, ʏᴏᴜ ᴄᴏᴍᴇ ʜᴇʀᴇ, ᴀɴᴅ ʏᴏᴜ ᴡᴏʀsʜɪᴘ ɪɴ ʜɪs ʟɪɢʜᴛ ғᴏʀᴇᴠᴇʀ, ɴᴇᴠᴇʀ ғᴇᴇʟɪɴɢ ᴀɴʏᴛʜɪɴɢ ʙᴜᴛ ᴘᴇᴀᴄᴇ… Oʀ ‘Tʜᴇ Sɪʟᴇɴᴄᴇ’ ᴀs ᴡᴇ ʟɪᴋᴇ ᴛᴏ ᴄᴀʟʟ ɪᴛ” Mark noticed that short of the wails and chants of worship, there were no other sounds in the city, apart from the conversation he was having now. It felt almost deafening. Normal silence was merely the absence of sound but this felt like its opposite. An all consuming nothingness that demanded his attention in the same way that a roaring explosion would. The only thing convincing him that sound itself still existed were the wails and autonomous chants of worship, which in turn replaced the normal, and became a form of silence in themselves. “Nᴏ ᴍᴏʀᴇ ᴅɪsᴛʀᴀᴄᴛɪᴏɴs, ɴᴏ ᴍᴏʀᴇ ғᴇᴀʀ ᴏʀ sᴇʟғɪsʜ ᴛʜᴏᴜɢʜᴛs ɢᴇᴛᴛɪɴɢ ɪɴ ᴛʜᴇ ᴡᴀʏ. Jᴜsᴛ ɴᴇᴠᴇʀ-ᴇɴᴅɪɴɢ ʟᴏᴠᴇ ғʀᴏᴍ ᴛʜᴇ ʟɪɢʜᴛ, ᴀɴᴅ ᴄᴏᴍᴘʟᴇᴛᴇ ᴘᴇᴀᴄᴇ, ᴡɪᴛʜᴏᴜᴛ ᴛʜᴇ ᴛᴜʀᴍᴏɪʟ ᴛʜᴀᴛ sᴛᴇᴍs ғʀᴏᴍ ᴇᴍᴏᴛɪᴏɴ ᴀɴᴅ ᴛʜᴏᴜɢʜᴛ! Eᴠᴇʀʏᴛʜɪɴɢ ʏᴏᴜ ᴘᴇᴏᴘʟᴇ ᴡᴇʀᴇ ᴘʀᴏᴍɪsᴇᴅ, ᴀɴᴅ ᴡᴀɴᴛᴇᴅ” Mark wasn’t sure, but he thought it was smiling. That just made it all the more terrible to behold. Then it dawned on him that, despite knowing his eyes were gone, the being in front of him was imprinted in his mind, clear as ever. He paused as he turned his head, and saw the otherworldly light of the… Angels, he supposed, and the burning rivers and trees, pierce the darkness of his blindness. The silhouette of the black pearl city was somehow a darker shade of black in his mind, looming in the background. All around, he could make out the shape of the city and its fiery inhabitants, but the worshippers were but shadows now, flickering wisps of darkness amongst the golden flames. His heart sank, as he realised the awful truth of his situation: He truly was dead, and whatever afterlife he’d been promised in life, they’d gotten a few crucial details very wrong. “Can I…” he began. “Yᴇs?” The Clerk replied, not unkindly. “Can I ask what happens to the others?” Mark finally managed. “To the sinners?”. “Aʜ, ʏᴏᴜ ᴍᴇᴀɴ ᴛʜᴏsᴇ ᴡʜᴏ ʟɪᴠᴇᴅ ʟɪᴠᴇs ʀᴜʟᴇᴅ ʙʏ ᴏ̨ᴜᴇsᴛɪᴏɴs ᴀɴᴅ ᴛʜᴇ ᴡʜɪᴍs ᴏғ ᴛʜᴇ ʜᴇᴀʀᴛ, ᴀɴᴅ ᴅᴇsɪʀᴇ?” Mark swallowed when he heard it phrased like that. “Yes? You said everyone comes here?”. “Wᴇʟʟ ɴᴏ ᴏʙᴠɪᴏᴜsʟʏ ɴᴏᴛ. Oɴʟʏ Hɪs ғʟᴏᴄᴋ ᴄᴏᴍᴇs ʜᴇʀᴇ. Mᴏsᴛ ᴅᴏɴ’ᴛ ɢᴏ ᴀɴʏᴡʜᴇʀᴇ ᴀᴛ ᴀʟʟ, ʙᴜᴛ ᴇᴠᴇʀʏᴏɴᴇ ɢᴏᴇs ᴡʜᴇʀᴇ ᴛʜᴇʏ ᴍᴇᴀɴ ᴛᴏ. A ʟɪғᴇ ᴏғ ᴏʙᴇᴅɪᴇɴᴄᴇ ᴀɴᴅ ᴡᴏʀsʜɪᴘ ʟᴇᴀᴅs ᴛᴏ ᴀɴ ᴇᴛᴇʀɴɪᴛʏ ᴏғ ᴏʙᴇᴅɪᴇɴᴄᴇ ᴀɴᴅ ᴡᴏʀsʜɪᴘ. Dᴇsɪʀᴇ ʟᴇᴀᴅs ᴛᴏ ᴅᴇsɪʀᴇ. Iᴛ ᴡᴀs ᴅᴇsɪɢɴᴇᴅ ᴛᴏ ᴀʟʟᴏᴡ ʏᴏᴜʀ ʟɪғᴇ ᴛᴏ ʟᴇᴀᴅ ᴛᴏ ᴛʜᴇ ᴀғᴛᴇʀʟɪғᴇ ʏᴏᴜ ᴡᴀɴᴛᴇᴅ.” the Clerk tilted its head and attempted a look that Mark suspected was supposed to be sympathy, but with a dozen eyes at once and he suddenly felt a bit squeamish. “Iᴛ’s ᴀʟʟ ᴠᴇʀʏ ᴍᴇᴛᴀᴘʜʏsɪᴄᴀʟ” it carried on. “Sɪɴᴄᴇ ᴡᴏʀsʜɪᴘ ғᴜᴇʟs ᴇᴠᴇʀʏᴛʜɪɴɢ, ᴇᴀᴄʜ ᴀғᴛᴇʀʟɪғᴇ ɪs ᴍᴀᴅᴇ ᴛᴏ ғɪᴛ ᴛʜᴇ ʙᴇʟɪᴇғs ᴏғ ᴛʜᴏsᴇ ᴡʜᴏ ᴇɴᴛᴇʀ, ᴇɴsᴜʀɪɴɢ ᴀ ᴄᴏɴsᴛᴀɴᴛ sᴜᴘᴘʟʏ ᴏғ ᴡᴏʀsʜɪᴘ ɪɴ ɪᴛ’s ᴏᴡɴ ᴡᴀʏ, ᴡʜɪᴄʜ ᴜʟᴛɪᴍᴀᴛᴇʟʏ ᴋᴇᴇᴘs ᴛʜᴇ ʟɪɢʜᴛs ᴏɴ ᴀʀᴏᴜɴᴅ ʜᴇʀᴇ”. “Lights? You mean it keeps the universe running?” Mark said, with uncertain hope. Some semblance of a promise that it was at least not all for naught, if it fueled all of creation itself. “Hᴍʜ? Oʜ ʜᴇᴀᴠᴇɴs ɴᴏ. Jᴜsᴛ ᴛʜᴇ ʟɪɢʜᴛs. Iᴛ ᴛᴀᴋᴇs ᴀ ʟᴏᴛ ᴛᴏ ғᴜᴇʟ ᴀʟʟ ᴛʜɪs ғɪʀᴇ, ʙᴜᴛ ᴡᴇ ғᴏᴜɴᴅ ᴛʜᴀᴛ ᴘʀᴀʏᴇʀ ʙᴜʀɴs ʙʀɪɢʜᴛᴇʀ ᴛʜᴀɴ ᴀɴʏ ᴏᴛʜᴇʀ sᴜʙsᴛᴀɴᴄᴇ. Nᴏ ᴛʜᴇ ᴜɴɪᴠᴇʀsᴇ ᴊᴜsᴛ ɢᴇᴛs ᴏɴ ᴡɪᴛʜ ɪᴛs ᴏᴡɴ ᴛʜɪɴɢ. Iᴛ ᴅᴏᴇsɴ’ᴛ ʀᴇᴀʟʟʏ ᴄᴀʀᴇ ᴡʜᴀᴛ ʏᴏᴜ ɢᴇᴛ ᴜᴘ ᴛᴏ” If the Clerk noticed Mark wince at the notion that nothing he’d ever done mattered, it made no show of it. “Aɴʏᴡᴀʏ, ᴀs ᴍᴜᴄʜ ᴀs I’ᴅ ʟᴏᴠᴇ ᴛᴏ sɪᴛ ᴀɴᴅ ᴛᴀʟᴋ, ᴛʜᴇʀᴇ’s ᴀ ʙɪᴛ ᴏғ ᴀ ʟɪɴᴇ ʙᴜɪʟᴅɪɴɢ ʙᴇʜɪɴᴅ ʏᴏᴜ. I ʙᴇʟɪᴇᴠᴇ ᴀ ᴘᴀɴᴅᴇᴍɪᴄ ᴊᴜsᴛ ʜɪᴛ ᴀ ʀᴜʀᴀʟ ᴀʀᴇᴀ, ᴀɴᴅ ɪᴛ sᴇᴇᴍs ᴛʜᴀᴛ “ᴛʜᴏᴜɢʜᴛs ᴀɴᴅ ᴘʀᴀʏᴇʀs” ᴅɪᴅ ɪᴛs ᴜsᴜᴀʟ ᴊᴏʙ, sᴏ ᴡᴇ’ʀᴇ ᴀʙᴏᴜᴛ ᴛᴏ ʜᴀᴠᴇ ᴀɴ ɪɴғʟᴜx ᴏғ ɴᴇᴡ ᴡᴏʀsʜɪᴘᴘᴇʀs ᴄᴏᴍɪɴɢ ɪɴ. Wᴏʀᴋ ɴᴇᴠᴇʀ ᴇɴᴅs ᴇʜ?” The Clerk remarked jovially, as it returned to its great book. “Nᴇxᴛ ᴘʟᴇᴀsᴇ! Wᴇʟᴄᴏᴍᴇ ᴍᴏʀᴛᴀʟ, ɪғ ʏᴏᴜ’ʟʟ ᴘʟᴇᴀsᴇ sᴛᴀᴛᴇ ʏᴏᴜʀ ɴᴀᴍᴇ?” It said to someone behind him, and Mark felt a strange compulsion to advance through the black marble gates. He heard a voice exclaim “But this can’t be right! I’m in the wrong place!” but he didn’t bother turning around to see who else was about to realise that dying wasn’t the worst thing to happen to them today. Mark stepped forward, feeling his ability to resist fading with each step. Before him, the Lamb turned around to greet him, a gentle smile on the same face Mark had seen on wooden ornaments and pictures throughout his life. The eyes were heavy, but kind, and he felt His love wash over him. With a sigh, he surrendered to the light, letting go of the last remnants of thought or questions. Slowly, the only thing in his mind was silence, his heart purged of desires both good and bad. His empty eye sockets turned toward the light at the centre of the marble city, his words the tortured chants of praise and worship.
